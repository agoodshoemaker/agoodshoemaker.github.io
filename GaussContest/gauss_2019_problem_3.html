Section 1.3 Problem-3

¶

Inside a square of area \(36\ cm^2\text{,}\) there are shaded regions as shown.

The ratio of the shaded area to the unshaded area is \(3:1\text{.}\) What is the value of \(a + b + c + d\) where \(a, b, c, d\) are the lengths of the bases of the shaded regions? Further, if three of \(a, b, c, d\) are equal integers and one different, then find them.

Hint

Dividing the number by \(9\) is a good start.

Solution

We obtain four triangles from the shaded area. The sum of the area of these shaded triangles is:

\begin{equation*} \cfrac{1}{2} \cdot a \cdot 6 + \Bigg( \cfrac{1}{2} \cdot b \cdot 6 + \cfrac{1}{2} \cdot c \cdot 6 \Bigg) + \cfrac{1}{2} \cdot d \cdot 6 = \cfrac{1}{2} \cdot 6 \cdot (a + b + c + d) \end{equation*}

As the ratio of the shaded to unshaded area is \(3:1\text{,}\) the area of the shaded region will be \(\frac{3}{4} \times 36 = 27\ cm^2\text{.}\) With this, we get:

\begin{equation*} \cfrac{1}{2} \cdot 6 \cdot (a + b + c + d) = 27 \implies a + b + c + d = 9 \end{equation*}

As for the second part of the question, some three integers are equal. Let the four numbers be \((x, x, x, y)\text{.}\) Then, we have:

\begin{equation*} 3x + y = 9 \implies y = 9 - 3x \end{equation*}

\(x\) can only take values \(1\) or \(2\) as we want \(y > 0\text{.}\) For \(x = 1\text{,}\) we get \(y = 6\text{.}\) But the side of the square is itself of length \(6\text{.}\) So this solution is ruled out. For \(x = 2\text{,}\) we get \(y = 3\text{.}\) So the four sides are \((2, 2, 2, 3)\text{.}\) Note that the order doesn't matter.
