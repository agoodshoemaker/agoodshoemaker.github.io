Consider two positive real numbers $a$ and $b$. Then the following inequality holds:

$$
\sqrt{\cfrac{a^2 + b^2}{2}} \geq \cfrac{a + b}{2} \geq \sqrt{ab} \geq \cfrac{2ab}{a + b}
$$

The equality occurs when $a = b$. This an inequality that brings together four ways of taking a mean of the two numbers:

- Quadratic mean: $\sqrt{\cfrac{a^2 + b^2}{2}}$
- Arithmetic mean: $\cfrac{a + b}{2}$
- Geometric mean: $\sqrt{ab}$
- Harmonic mean: $\cfrac{2ab}{a + b}$
