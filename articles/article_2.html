The Power of the Medium

The media that are used to transmit ideas will be the subject of our discussion. I have kept the definition of an idea fairly broad - a musical composition is a musical idea, a novel is a literary idea, a business plan is a strategic idea. Without stepping too far into the metaphysics of ideas, it is safe to conjecture that ideas inhabit a realm of the mental universe that is common to all. Science has had quite a few occassions when two different minds have independently discovered the same idea. Darwin and Wallace, Newton and Leibniz, are typical examples that lend credence to the universality of ideas. Once in possession of an idea the human mind seeks to export it in a consumable form in the market of ideas so that other minds can join in this mental commerce. The medium is the platform that enables this interchange. This essay is concerned with the effect the medium has on ideas. The central question is : does the medium modify the idea in its passage from the originating mind, or shall we say the discovering mind, to the receiving mind?

Is a book better than a computer for reading? Or is this just mental conditioning? To answer this question, we can define a purity-index for a medium - it is a measure of its integrity to the function it is meant to satisfy. A printed book has a very high level of purity: it serves exactly one function, nothing more, nothing less, and it serves this function perfectly. The most impure medium is WhatsApp. WhatsApp is the digital commons of the old and the young alike, the preeminent medium for information interchange. We share photos, music, videos, quotes, short-stories; make audio and video calls; use it to stalk people, friends and foes alike. In short, it supports every form of human communication other than the physical. This dimishes the power of the ideas that go through it. The expressive-bandwidth that is available with the medium is shared between all types of ideas. WhatsApp means so many things to a user, and trying to serve all these functions affects the quality of reception of any single idea. There is an undesirable conflation of functions. Any idea transmitted through it starts losing its power and becomes yet another WhatsApp message. The ebook is another case in point. The reason for the backlash against the rise e-books may not be limited to the strong tendency of conservatism that prevents us from exploring other media. E-books reside in the web and are accessed through digital devices. Again, these devices are used for so many other things, and consequently have a low purity.

The ideal medium should hold a faithful mirror to the idea that seeks expression through it. A book does this; it is the physical expression of a particular idea and it serves no other purpose. But it fulfills its assigned role exceptionally well. As technolgosits try to invent more and more general purpose devices that replace existing media, we must pause and question what we are losing in the process.
