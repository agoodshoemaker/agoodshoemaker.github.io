Divide a triangle into four smaller triangles of equal area using three line segments.

<!--more-->

<hr>

There are three possible ways to achieve the desired result. We use two different properties of triangles to get the required objects.<br><br>

**Median:** The median divides the triangle into two smaller triangles with equal area. After drawing the  median to a base, we can either divide the base again or bisect the median.<br><br>

By dividing the base into four equal segments, i.e., $BD = DE = EF = FC$, the required triangles of equal area are $ABD, ADE, AEF, AFC$. This is shown in the following figure:

	<figcaption>Fig-1</figcaption>
	<br>
</figure>

By bisecting the median and dropping a median to the median, we get $AD = DE$ and $BE = EC$. The required triangles are $ADB, BDE, ADC, EDC$. This is shown in the following figure:

<figure>
	<img src="/assets/problems/problem-8/i2.png" width="130%" height="130%" />
	<figcaption>Fig-2</figcaption>
	<br>
</figure>

  


**Medial triangle:** The lines joining the midpoints of sides divide the triangle into four congruent triangles.

	<img src="/assets/problems/problem-8/i3.png" width="130%" height="130%" />

All three constructions have made use of exactly three line segments as stipulated in the question.
