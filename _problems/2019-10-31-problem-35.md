**(AMTI-2004-SubJunior-Finals)** Three counters $A, B$ and $C$ are colored with three different colors red, blue and white. Of the following statements only one is true.

1. $A$ is red.
2. $B$ is not red.
3. $C$ is not blue.

What is the color of each counter?

<!--more-->

<hr><br>

Three cases to consider.

1. First statement is true: In this case, $A$ is red, $B$ is also red and $C$ is blue as the second and third statements are false. As all three have to get a different color, the first statement cannot be true.
2. Second statement is true: In this case, $B$ could be blue or white, $A$ cannot be red while $C$ is blue. As both $A$ cannot be red, it has to be white. But this will leave $B$ with red, while $B$ cannot be red. The second statement also cannot be true.
3. Third statement is true: In this case, $C$ can be red or white. $B$ has to be red. $A$ could be white or blue. As $B$ is red, $C$ has to be white which leaves $A$ with blue. This is the true statement.

Final assignment of colors: $A$ : blue, $B$ : red, $C$ : white
