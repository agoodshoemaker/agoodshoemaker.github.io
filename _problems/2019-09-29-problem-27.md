**(AMTI-2014-SubJunior-Final**) Prove that the feet of the perpendicular drawn from the vertices of a parallelogram onto its diagonals are the vertices of another parallelogram.

<!--more-->

<hr><br>

Let $ABCD$ be the parallelogram and $E, F, G, H$ be the feet of the perpendiculars. We need to prove that $EFGH$ is also a parallelogram.

<figure>
	<img src="/assets/problems/problem-27/i1.png" width="130%" height="130%" />
	<figcaption>Nested Parallelogram</figcaption>
	<br>
</figure>

In triangles $AOE$ and $COG$, we have:

$$
\begin{align*}
&\boldsymbol{(A)}\ \ \angle AOE = \angle COG\\
&\boldsymbol{(S)}\ \ OA = OC\\
&\boldsymbol{(A)}\ \ \angle OAE = \angle OCG\\
\end{align*}
$$

From this we get $OE = OG$. Similarly we can prove that $OH = OF$. In quadrilateral $EFGH$, we have the diagonals $EG$ and $HF$ bisect each other. It follows that $EFGH$ is a parallelogram.<br><br>

**Notes**:

- $EFGH$ will degenerate into a point when $ABCD$ is a rhombus as the diagonals are perpendicular to each other.
- If the angle made by one of the diagonals with a side is obtuse, then the parallelogram $EFGH$ will have two of its vertices outside $ABCD$.
