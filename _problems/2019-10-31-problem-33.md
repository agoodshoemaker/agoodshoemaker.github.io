**(AMTI-2004-SubJunior-Finals)** Is the statement "If $p$ and $p^2 + 2$ are primes then $p^3 + 2$ is also a prime" true or false? Give reasons for your answer.


<!--more-->

<hr><br>

If $p$ is a prime greater than $3$, then $p^2 + 2$ is a multiple of $3$ and can never be a prime. So the "if" part of the statement implies that $p = 3$. In this case $p^3 + 2 = 29$ is a prime. Therefore this statement is always true!<br><br>
