Prove that area of the square erected on the hypotenuse of a right triangle is equal to the sum of the area of the squares erected on its legs.

<!--more-->
<hr><br>

The famous Pythagoras theorem. If there's one result in mathematics that has universal appeal it must be this. Sadly, many of us finish schooling without getting to know a proof of this theorem. There is a reason I have used the article "a" instead of "the" before the word proof in the last sentence because several proofs of the theorem have been discovered over the centuries. Here we will discuss the one given by Euclid in his book the *Elements*.
