A horizontal line $XX^{'}$ divides the plane into two half-planes, say "up" and "down". We choose to work with "up" and discard "down". Henceforth all objects lie in "up". $A$ and $B$ are two points on the line $XX^{'}$ and $AB$ is the diameter of a semicircle with radius $AB = 2r$. Consider the following process for generating a set of triangles, $S$. Initially $S = \phi$:

- $CD$ is a chord of length $l$ inside the semicircle such that $AC$ and $BD$ do not intersect inside the circle.
- $AC$ and $BD$ when extended intersect at $P$ outside the circle.
- Add $\bigtriangleup APB$ to $S$.
- Continue this process by changing the orientation of $CD$ with respect to $AB$ but without changing its length.

For some triangle $APB$ in set $S$, the angle opposite $AB$ is $\theta$. Now answer the following:<br><br>

$(1)$ Prove that for every triangle in this set, the angle opposite the side $AB$ is some acute angle $\theta$.<br><br>
$(2)$ If $O^{'}$ is the circumcenter of triangle $PCD$, prove that $O, C, O^{'}, D$ are concyclic.<br><br>
$(3)$ Prove that the diameter of the circle through $OCO^{'}D$ is: $OO^{'} = r \cdot  \sqrt{ \Bigg(\cfrac{l}{2r}\Bigg)^4 + 1}$.<br><br>
$(4)$ Prove that there exists a triangle $APB$ with $\angle APB = \theta$ which is not in $S$. <br><br>
$(5)$ Let $L$ be the locus of the points $P$ generated by this process. Let $L_{\theta}$ be the locus of the points $P$ such that $\bigtriangleup APB$ has $\angle APB = \theta$.  If both these loci are considered as curves in the half-plane "up", what fraction of $L_{\theta}$ is $L$?

- If the length of $CD$ stays constant, then $\angle COD$ will also remain constant. From this we can infer that $\theta$ is a constant as long as the length of $CD$ is fixed and that it is independent of the orientation of $CD$ with $AB$.

### Part - 3

- For this part, let us first draw the segment connecting the centers.

<figure>
	<img src="/assets/problems/problem-101/i11.svg" width="100%" height="100%" />
	<br>
</figure>

- As $OO^{'}$ is the line joining the center of two intersecting circles, it bisects the common chord $CD$ perpendicularly. In the circumcircle of $OCO^{'}D$, chord $CD$ is bisected perpendicularly by $OO^{'}$, hence it is the diameter of this circle. Also, the line joining the centers is the bisector of angles $\angle COD$ and $\angle CO^{'}$. So the two triangles $ODO^{'}$ and $OCO^{'}$ are right triangles.

<figure>
	<img src="/assets/problems/problem-101/i13.svg" width="100%" height="100%" />
	<br>
</figure>

- Now for the distance $OO^{'}$. Let $O^{'}D = r^{'}$. Then we have:

$$
(OO^{'})^{2} = r^2 + (r^{'})^{2}
$$

- Also, in right triangle $ODO^{'}$, the altitude to the hypotenuse $DE$ is the geometric mean of the two legs. So:

$$
\Bigg( \cfrac{l}{2} \Bigg)^2 = r \cdot r^{'} \implies r^{'} = \cfrac{l^2}{4r}
$$

- Using this in the previous equation, we get:



$$
\begin{align*}
(OO^{'})^{2} &= r^2 + (r^{'})^{2}\\ \\
&= r^2 + \cfrac{l^4}{16r^2}\\ \\
&= r^2 \Bigg(1 + \cfrac{l^4}{16r^4} \Bigg) \\ \\
\implies OO^{'} &= r \cdot \sqrt{1 + \Bigg( \cfrac{l}{2r} \Bigg)^4}
\end{align*}
$$

### Part - 5

- The chord $CD$ of length $l$ can occupy two extreme positions, one on the right where $D$ merges with $B$ and the other on the left when $C$ merges with $A$. As $C$ approaches this limiting configuration, the line $AC$ will become a tangent at $A$, in the other extreme the line $BD$ will become tangent at $B$ as $D$ approaches $B$. Call the points produced by the intersection of $AC$ and $BD$ in either case as $P_l$ and $P_r$. Note that these two extreme points are reached in the limit.

<figure>
	<img src="/assets/problems/problem-101/i17.svg" width="100%" height="100%" />
	<br>
</figure>

- The locus $L$ is therefore the arc $\widehat{P_lP_r}$ of circle $\omega_2$ and the locus $L_{\theta}$ is the arc $\widehat{AB}$ of circle $\omega_2$. The desired ratio is therefore:

$$
\cfrac{l(\widehat{P_lP_r})}{l(\widehat{AB})}
$$

- Now notice that the point of intersection of segments $AP_r$ and $BP_l$ is the center of the circle $\omega_2$. This is because $P_lB$ and $P_rA$ are diameters of the circle and their point of intersection has to be the center. Mark it as $O^{'}$. 

<figure>
	<img src="/assets/problems/problem-101/i18.svg" width="100%" height="100%" />
	<br>
</figure>

- With that we can make the final observation that $\angle P_l O^{'} P_r = 2 \theta$. 

<figure>
	<img src="/assets/problems/problem-101/i19.svg" width="100%" height="100%" />
	<br>
</figure>

- Therefore the ratio becomes:

$$
\cfrac{l(\widehat{P_lP_r})}{l(\widehat{AB})} = \cfrac{2 \theta}{360^{\circ} - 2 \theta} = \cfrac{1}{\cfrac{360^{\circ}}{2 \theta} - 1}
$$

- As $\theta$ is acute, $0 < 2 \theta < 180^{\circ}$. This means the ratio will indeed lie between $0$ and $1$.
