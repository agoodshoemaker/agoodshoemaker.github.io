**Integer triangles**: Find the number of distinct triangles with integral sides having a perimeter of $n$ units, where $n$ is any positive integer greater than or equal to $3$.

<!--more-->
<hr><br>

Let the three sides of the triangle be $L, M, S$ with $L \geq M \geq S$. The following inequality must hold for the longest side:

$$
\left\lfloor \cfrac{n + 2}{3} \right \rfloor \leq L \leq \left\lfloor \cfrac{n - 1}{2} \right\rfloor
$$

Here $\left \lfloor x \right\rfloor$ represents the greatest integer less than or equal to $x$. This inequality can be readily obtained from the triangle inequality applied to the triangle with $L$ as the longest side and $n$ as the perimeter. With that, we have to solve:

$$
M + S = n - L
$$

with $1 \leq S \leq M \leq L$, where $M , S, L$ are all positive integers.
